I just told my mom that I want to sign up for STEP and take the exam in Chengdu or Chongqing. She said that given our family’s situation, the round-trip transportation fees, exam registration cost and hotel expenses are not a small sum. She asked me if I was certain and confident enough to take the exam. I said yes, but I definitely had no guarantee of what score I would get.

She then asked if I could even finish studying all the material. I promised I would, and that I could also set aside plenty of time to do practice questions. She asked again if this wasn’t just for me to experiment and make mistakes. I told her I had no chance to afford trial and error anymore. I asked if she would agree to let me go, and she said she was very hesitant. I said okay and left.

Then she said, “If I don’t agree, we just have nothing to talk about, right?” A minute later, I walked back and handed her my lucky money from the Spring Festival, telling her to transfer it to me so I could use it, then left again.

I still want to earn money on my own under these circumstances, even if I can barely keep up with my studies. Please help me. How can I make money? I’m actually really upset.

Our family isn’t that badly off, but my mom refuses to sell that vacant house, as if the million-yuan paper loss wouldn’t exist if she didn’t sell it. I feel so wronged.

I once watched a movie when I was in junior high—skipped my cram school class to see it: Goodbye, Li Kele. The main character’s family was just a street food stall selling grilled skewers, yet her father was willing to take her skiing in Xinjiang and tried his best to fulfill all her wishes.

Okay, it’s true that maybe the trust between my parents and me isn’t as strong as theirs. But I really envy them, so badly.

And after her father passed away, her mother said, “Keep moving forward, don’t look back.” That line both relieved me and made me cry.

Then I suddenly realized that I don’t actually care how much money my family has. I just want them to make me feel firmly and surely that I am worthy.

Only children who can’t have what they want have to hold themselves up again and again, comfort themselves over and over. Only a lonely wolf cub has to lick its own wounds. Those sheltered by their parents would never get hurt in the first place.
